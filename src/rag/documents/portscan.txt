Port Scanning is a reconnaissance technique used by attackers to send packets to specific ports on a host to discover active services, open ports, and operating system vulnerabilities.

Impacts:
- Exposure of active network services
- System architecture mapping by malicious actors
- Identification of unpatched software entry points
- Increased risk of targeted exploitation

Mitigations:
- Configuring firewalls to block unauthorized inbound scanning
- Disabling unused network ports and services
- Implementing Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS) to detect and drop port scan traffic
- Utilizing port knocking techniques for sensitive administrative services
